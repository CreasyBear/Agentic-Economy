# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-06-27*

## Product Overview

**One-liner:** Agentic Economy gives Australian local businesses a controlled public front door for AI-era discovery.

**What it does:** A business owner can claim and publish a public business page, expose truthful registry/search and agent-discovery projections, and see whether that discovery path is healthy. The first product is claim, publish, discover, and observe. Conversation, protected actions, payments, and autonomous execution are future phases.

**Product category:** Local business discovery and agent-readable business registry. Future category: agentic commerce control plane for real-world services.

**Product type:** B2B/B2SMB SaaS with public directory/discovery surfaces.

**Business model:** Phase 1 has no paid path. Future paid activation may use subscriptions and usage-based/provider-backed rails only after authority, receipt, and readback gates exist.

## Target Audience

**Target companies:** Australian local-service SMBs that rely on inbound demand and need their business facts, availability posture, and customer contact path to remain controlled as customers use AI assistants.

**Decision-makers:** Owner-operators, practice managers, office managers, franchise/local managers, and technical partners building agent-facing business directories.

**Primary use case:** Claim a controlled public front door so humans and AI agents can find accurate, source-owned business information.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Publish a business presence without waiting on ABN/ABR verification.
- Make business facts discoverable to search, registry, and AI-agent surfaces.
- See whether publication, indexing, and discovery are healthy.
- Prevent wrong, duplicate, impersonated, or overclaiming public records.

**Use cases:**
- Owner claims a business page from name/category/suburb/slug.
- Owner shares a public page URL.
- Agent/builder fetches a manifest and sees honest capabilities.
- Operator finds publish-but-not-indexed or manifest-degraded gaps.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** Local businesses do not control how they appear in AI-mediated discovery, and most directories do not expose trustworthy machine-readable state.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Search listings optimize for human snippets, not agent-readable capability/state.
- Generic directories often blur verified, claimed, callable, and paid states.
- Marketplaces create supply/demand theatre before the owner has a controlled front door.
- Protocol demos expose schemas without solving owner authority and operational health.

**What it costs them:** Missed demand, wrong customer expectations, duplicate listings, impersonation risk, and inability to prepare for agent-mediated customer flows.

**Emotional tension:** Owners want demand, but they do not want AI or platforms inventing facts about their business.

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct:** Local directories and business profile tools — fall short because they optimize listings, not source-owned agent discovery and operator-visible projection health.

**Secondary:** Google Business Profile and marketplace listings — fall short because they are platform-owned surfaces, not an AE-controlled discovery spine or future protected-action gateway.

**Indirect:** Website/contact-form-only presence — falls short because agents need structured, parseable, source-owned discovery and status.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
- Claim-before-verification T0 publishing.
- Discovery output generated from source-owned state.
- Honest status separation: claimed, published, indexed, discoverable, verified, callable, payable.
- Operator-visible index/discovery failures.
- Future-safe authority/audit posture before payments/actions.

**How we do it differently:** AE treats the business front door as the product and registry/discovery outputs as projections from durable source state, not hand-written marketing artifacts.

**Why that's better:** It avoids fake readiness, fake protocol claims, and marketplace bloat while preserving the future path to owner-approved actions and payments.

**Why customers choose us:** They want control and discoverability without handing their business identity to another marketplace before demand exists.

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "Is this just another directory?" | No. Phase 1 includes a directory-like projection, but the product is source-owned claim/publish/discovery health for AI-era demand. |
| "Why no ABN first?" | ABN/ABR is a later registry trust tier. T0 claim should not block a public front door. |
| "Can agents book/pay yet?" | No. Discoverable is not callable. Protected actions and payments require later authority, receipt, and provider-readback gates. |
| "Is this verified?" | Only if the displayed trust tier says so. Claimed pages are explicitly not registry-verified. |

**Anti-persona:** Anyone seeking a fake marketplace, scraped directory, automated booking/payment bot, or protocol demo without owner authority.

## Switching Dynamics

**Push:** Existing listings are stale, scattered, platform-owned, or not agent-readable.

**Pull:** One controlled front door and honest discovery health.

**Habit:** Owners already rely on Google/social/websites and may not see agent-mediated demand yet.

**Anxiety:** Fear of wrong claims, fake verification, extra admin burden, and being locked into another marketplace.
