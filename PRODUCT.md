# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Agentic Economy serves local urgent-service business owners, public operators, and discovery clients that need source-owned service facts. Owners arrive under operational pressure and need to claim, publish, correct, or remove a business page without being misled about unavailable bookings, payments, or automated actions. Operators and admins need a high-trust command surface for readbacks, degraded states, disputes, and source-write authority.

## Product Purpose

Agentic Economy is a source-owned civic command surface for local-service trust. It creates truthful public service pages and registry projections that customers and assistants can read, while clearly separating live capabilities from future agentic commerce rails. Success means a business page can be claimed, published, searched, corrected, and audited with every status grounded in source facts rather than speculative marketplace promises.

## Brand Personality

Calm, authoritative, exact. The product should feel operationally serious without becoming cold: a public signal board for service truth, not a generic SaaS catalog or an agent marketplace teaser.

## Anti-references

Avoid AI-purple gradients, fake dashboard previews, decorative glassmorphism, repeated three-card feature grids, hero metric templates, decorative status dots, colored side-stripes, and future-capability theatre. Do not imply payments, wallets, hosted agents, bookings, request markets, MCP, OpenAPI, or callable actions before the phase gates make them real. Do not call anything verified unless the trust tier is `registry_verified`.

## Design Principles

1. Make source ownership visible. Every public or owner-facing status should name what is known, what is unavailable, and where the fact came from.
2. Consequence before decoration. Elevation, dark command panels, and cobalt action color are reserved for decisions, readbacks, and primary action.
3. Tell the truth about capability. The UI must be ambitious about agentic commerce while being explicit about what is not live yet.
4. Use familiar product affordances for high-stakes work. Forms, badges, loading, empty, and error states should feel predictable and trustworthy.
5. Preserve civic calm. Distinctiveness comes from structure, hierarchy, and status architecture rather than visual noise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for public, owner, and admin surfaces. Status must never rely on color alone. Focus states stay visible, touch targets meet at least 44px where practical, form labels remain persistent, and reduced-motion users get static or instant alternatives for nonessential animation.
